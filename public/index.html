The scalability trilemma The scalability trilemma says that there are three properties that a blockchain try to have, and that, if you stick to “simple” techniques, you can only get two of those three. The three properties are: Scalability: the chain can process more transactions than a single regular node (think: a consumer laptop) can verify. Decentralization: the chain can run without any trust dependencies on a small group of large centralized actors.
